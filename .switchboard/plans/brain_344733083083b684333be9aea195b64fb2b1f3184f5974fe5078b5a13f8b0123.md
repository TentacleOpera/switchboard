### 🚨 Critical Funnel Leakage Points:
1. **Onboarding → Registration (67.1% Dropoff)**: We lose **967 users** during registration. This suggests significant friction in the sign-up flow (e.g., too many fields, lack of social login, or premature paywall placement).
2. **Registration → Purchase (94.5% Dropoff)**: Only **26 out of 475 registered users** converted to a paid subscription. This indicates a high willingness to register but low purchase intent or price resistance at the paywall stage.

## 🛠️ Actionable Takeaways & Next Steps

1. **UX/Product Audit: The Registration Flow**
   * *Objective*: Increase Onboarding-to-Registration conversion from 32.9%.
   * *Action*: Review the registration fields, simplify the UX, and test placing registration *after* users experience some in-app value rather than immediately after onboarding views.
2. **Technical Audit: Android Paywall & Billing Verification**
   * *Objective*: Resolve the iOS/Android subscription disparity (8 iOS vs. 1 Android).
   * *Action*: Launch a manual test of the Android app's purchase flow to verify that Google Play Billing is functional and that the paywall renders correctly on different Android screen sizes.
3. **Paywall & Offer Optimization**
   * *Objective*: Elevate the registered-to-purchase conversion from 5.5%.
   * *Action*: Test introducing a free trial option or a discount offer immediately following registration, and optimize the paywall's copy to better explain the premium value proposition.
4. **Investigate Zero-Usage Features**
   * *Objective*: Address product dead-zones (`therapist_open` and `question_submitted`).
   * *Action*: Verify if these entry points are broken in code or if they are hidden from the primary user navigation. If they are functional but ignored, consider deprecating them to simplify the app interface.
