# Index Page Copy Improvements

A section-by-section review of the current copy in [index.astro](file:///Users/patrickvuleta/Documents/GitHub/switchboard-site/src/pages/index.astro) — what's working, what I'd tighten, and proposed rewrites.

---

## Overall impressions

The voice is strong — direct, confident, avoids jargon — and the "0→1 arc" framing is clear and differentiating. The biggest opportunities:

1. **Some paragraphs run long and lose the reader mid-sentence.** The hero sub especially tries to cover too many ideas at once. Shorter rhythms land harder.
2. **Repeated phrases dilute impact.** "You already pay for" appears three times; "agents you already use" shows up in similar forms. The point is good, say it once with force, then vary the language.
3. **Some H3s are soft** — they state a fact rather than making a claim that earns a click. "CLI, hands-free" is flat; "Your terminals, on autopilot" carries more weight.
4. **A few body paragraphs over-explain the mechanism** when the reader just needs the payoff. Trust the docs links to carry the detail.
5. **The "before the code" section** could hit harder by naming the *pain* (context loss, planning outside the repo, work that never reaches the agent) before the solution.

**Changes:**
